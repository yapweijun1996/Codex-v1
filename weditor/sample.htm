3.0             SCOPE OF SERVICES

The engagement of IDA as lead consultant of the project shall be in accordance to the Conditions of Appointment and Architects services and mode of payment by Singapore Institute of Architects.

 

3.1              Schematic Design Phase

a)        Taking the Clients instructions to ascertain the requirements and constraints of the works;

b)       Preparing schematic sketch designs to interpret the design brief;

c)        To develop the designs to a stage sufficient to enable applications/submissions to be made to BCA, LTA, URA and other relevant authorities for planning approval required under any relevant laws and regulations;

d)       Coordinating drawings with the relevant authorities to obtain written planning permission;

e)        Prepare and develop the design brief for the Mechanical and Electrical installation:

The M&E services will generally include only the building services systems such as:-

·         ACMV installation

·         Electrical installation

·         Piped services installation

·         Fire Protection installation

·         Lift installation

·         Building Management system

f)         Provide cost planning advice and monitor evolving designs in relation to elemental cost targets;

g)        Preparing preliminary estimates of the probable construction costs (with the assistance of a qualified Quantity Surveyor) based on current area;

h)       Monitoring and reconciliation of all updated estimates and budgets prepared by all consultants and assimilation into the overall project cost plan;

i)         To submit construction phasing and timeline for the project;

j)         To attend regular consultants and client meetings.

 

3.2              Design Development Phase

Based on the proposal as approved by the Client and the relevant authority, this stage consists of:-

a)        Developing the schematic design drawings to a stage to enable the consultants to commence detailed design work;

b)       Preparing detailed design drawings to comply with the various relevant authorities requirements;

c)        Submitting the detailed design drawings together with other particulars to the Building and Construction Authority and other authorities to obtain statutory building approval;

d)       Updating the preliminary costs estimate (assisted by a qualified quantity surveyor) of probable construction costs;

e)        Preparing and updating a project schedule and submitting the same to the Client for approval.

 

3.3              Tender Documentation Phase

Based on the Quantity Surveyors updated costs estimate and the project schedule approved by the Client, this phase consists of:-

a)        Preparing and finalizing the working drawings, specifications and such other documents as may be necessary for the preparation of tender documents by a quantity surveyor;

b)       In collaboration with other consultants, preparing all documents necessary for inviting tenders for the project;

c)        Evaluating the tenders and submitting a report and recommendation to the Client;

d)       Awarding the contract on behalf of the Client;

e)        Collaborating with the quantity surveyor and other consultants as necessary in the preparation of the contract documents on behalf of the client and the building contractor.

 

3.4              Construction and Post Construction Phase

This stage consists of functions of the architect as defined in the SIA building contracts.

a)        Providing all necessary information and issuing instructions to the building contractor to enable the building contractor to proceed with the works;

b)       Examining the works programme prepared by the building contractor to ensure that the works can reasonably be completed within the contract period barring unforeseen circumstances but in so doing shall not be construed to have assumed responsibility for the building contractors works performance;

c)        To make periodic site inspection and ensure that construction work is carried out to approved drawings and make amendment to plans if necessary and issuing certificates as required under the building contract;

d)       To carry out site inspection and evaluate the works to ensure that it is in line with specification and approved drawings, compliance with UDG and TOP requirement;

e)        Accepting the building contract on behalf of the Client, providing a set of architectural drawings showing the building as constructed and obtaining for the Client the drawings of the buildings services as installed and the recommendations on their maintenance from the consultants and specialist Contractors;

f)         To prepare submission drawings for amendment plans to approve drawings prior to Temporary Occupation Permit (TOP) inspection;

g)        Applying for and obtaining TOP and Certificate of Statutory Completion (CSC) and endorsement on plan on completion from the relevant authorities;
